~ Featured Recipes ~

Paneer Tikka

Paneer tikka is a popular vegetarian appetizer or snack originating from North India. It consists of chunks of paneer, an Indian-style cottage cheese, marinated in yogurt and spices, and then grilled in a tandoor, a cylindrical clay oven. The paneer tikka is known for its smoky flavor and tender texture.

Veg Manchurian

Veg Manchurian is a beloved Indo-Chinese appetizer featuring crispy fried vegetable balls. A mix of shredded veggies are coated in batter, deep-fried, and tossed in a sweet, spicy, and tangy sauce. It's enjoyed dry or with a light gravy, often served alongside fried rice or noodles.

Garlic Bread

Garlic bread, also known as garlic toast, is a popular dish that consists of bread (usually a baguette, sourdough, or ciabatta) topped with garlic and occasionally olive oil or butter. It may also include additional herbs like oregano or chives. The bread is either grilled until toasted or baked in a conventional or bread oven.

White Sauce Pasta

White Sauce Pasta, also known as Bechamel Sauce Pasta, is a popular dish that consists of pasta mixed with a smooth, creamy, and delicious white sauce. The white sauce, also known as Bechamel sauce, is made of milk, butter, and flour. It originates from Italian and French cuisines.

Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular street food dish from Mumbai, India. It consists of a thick vegetable curry (bhaji) served with a soft bread roll (pav). The bhaji is a spiced mixture of mashed vegetables in a thick gravy. Vegetables in the curry may commonly include potatoes, onions, carrots, chillies, peas, bell peppers, and tomatoes.


~ Sweet Dishes ~

Soan Papdi

Soan Papdi, also known as san papri, shompapri, sohan papdi, shonpapdi or patisa, is a popular dessert in the Indian subcontinent. It is made of gram flour (besan), all-purpose flour, ghee, sugar, and milk. It is usually cube-shaped or served as flakes, and has a crisp and flaky texture.

Ras Gulla

Rasgulla is a syrupy dessert from eastern South Asia. It's like cheese dumplings! They are made with balls of dough from a special kind of cheese called chenna, cooked in a light sugar syrup. The result is soft, spongy balls that are light and sweet. It's a popular dessert in India, Bangladesh, and other parts of the region.

Mysore Pak

Mysore pak is a traditional South Indian sweet originating from the Mysore Palace (now Mysuru Palace) in the state of Karnataka, India. It's a dense fudge-like sweet made with gram flour (besan), ghee, and sugar. The richness of the dish comes from the ghee, which is clarified butter.

Chocolava Cake

A choco lava cake is a small, decadent dessert with a molten chocolate center. The cake itself is soft and cakey, but when you spoon into it, warm, rich chocolate oozes out. It's a delicious and impressive treat that's surprisingly easy to make at home.

Ras Malai

Rasmalai is a popular Indian dessert known for its juicy cheese balls and creamy sauce. It's made with chenna (Indian cottage cheese) kneaded into balls, cooked in sugar syrup, and then soaked in rich, thickened milk flavored with cardamom and nuts. The name itself comes from Hindi, with "ras" meaning "juice" and "malai" meaning "cream".
